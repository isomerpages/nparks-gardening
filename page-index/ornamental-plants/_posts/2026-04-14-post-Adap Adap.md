<h3>Adap-Adap (<em>Mussaenda glabra</em>)</h3>
<p><strong>Other common names:</strong> Balik Adap, Belak Adap, Cha Pedi,
Daun Puteri, Segoreh, 光叶玉叶金花</p>
<p>
<br>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" title="Adap-Adap flowers. Photo by Victoria Lim." src="/images/Plants/adapadap%20(2)_victorialim.jpg">
</div>
<p>Adap-Adap is a hardy <a href="/learn-more-about-gardening/glossary/#f" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">free-flowering</a> shrub
that is commonly seen in rewilding areas, nature parks, in roadside gardens,
and biodiversity-attracting gardens. This plant is also <a href="/page-index/glossary/native-plants/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">native</a> to
Singapore and is naturally found growing along freshwater swamp forest
and secondary forests. Its brightly coloured flowers attract our native
pollinators such as butterflies, while its fruits are a food source for
native wildlife.</p>
<p>A <a href="/learn-more-about-gardening/glossary/#p" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">perennial</a> flower,
Adap-Adap is useful as <a href="/page-index/glossary/biodiversity-attracting-plants/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">biodiversity-attracting plants</a> and
perimeter plants to add a pop of colour to any garden.</p>
<p>
<br>
</p>
<h4>Sun and soil needs:</h4>
<p>This plant thrives in <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/gauging-light/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">4-8 hours of direct sunlight or at least 6 hours of indirect sunlight</a>.
Plants do best in <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/planting-in-containers/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">pots</a> with
<a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/soil/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">loamy soil</a>at least 30cm deep, or in <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/true-ground/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">true ground</a>.
These plants are vulnerable to <a href="/page-index/plant-problems/root-rot/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">root rot</a>, so ensure that
your soil has plenty of <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/soil-amendments/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">organic matter</a> to
let the roots breathe.</p>
<p>
<br>
</p>
<h4>Growing:</h4>
<p>Adap-Adap are steady growers and generally does not need fertilising.
The shrub can grow up to 1m in height and will need to be pruned regularly.
<a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/pruning/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Regular pruning</a>to promote airflow will keep the plant healthy and
neat.</p>
<p>
<br>
</p>
<h4>Propagation:</h4>
<p>Adap-Adap can be grown from <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/propagating-by-seed/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">seed</a> or
<a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/propagating-by-cuttings/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">woody cuttings</a>with rooting hormone.</p>
<p>
<br>
</p>
<h4>Common problems &amp; solutions:</h4>
<p>This plant is relatively resistant to pests and disease if kept healthy.</p>
<p>If your plant has few to no flowers, you can fertilise the plant with
<a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/fertilising/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">fertilisers high in phosphorus and potassium</a>.</p>
<p>If the plant does not receive enough light, it will stop producing flowers.
<a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/pruning/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Prune</a>surrounding plants or move the plant to a sunnier spot to promote
flower growth.</p>
<p><a href="/page-index/pests/aphids/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Aphids</a>,
<a href="/page-index/pests/mealy-bugs/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Mealy Bugs</a>, <a href="/page-index/pests/whiteflies/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Whiteflies</a>,
and <a href="/page-index/pests/spider-mites/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Spider Mites</a> often
infest the plant if it has <a href="/learn-more-about-gardening/plant-problems/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">underlying problems</a> like
repeated wilting from heat stress. <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/pest-control/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Mechanical pest control</a> methods
like <a href="/page-index/horticulture-techniques/pruning/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">pruning</a> the
infested parts are the best methods for managing these pests in the short
term, but resolving the underlying problem will prevent them in the long
term.</p>
